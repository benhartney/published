Levels

Note: The following will not work on mobile.

We need to improve how we think about and communicate around politics. In this demo I will sketch out a way to represent someone's political views in a dashboard, to make political views clearer and easier to communicate. It includes three components of a political view:

  • The outcomes the person cares about
    • e.g. Bernie Sanders cares about income inequality
  • For each of those outcomes, the other policy metrics that impact it
    • e.g. Bernie Sanders thinks that the minimum wage level impacts income inequality
  • The various projections on the impact of a proposed policy change, and which projections the view holder believes
    • e.g. Various groups create projections on the outcomes of a proposal to increase the federal minimum wage, Bernie Sanders agrees with some of them but disagrees with others

For people who already write about and debate policy, the use cases of the dashboard are:

  • A clearer, more efficient, always up-to-date view on how society is doing (according to their view)
  • An easy way to share their view and compare it to others
  • An easy way to see the various projections for a policy proposal

If those use cases are validated, it may be possible to create use cases for less policy-minded people.

Finally, in some situations, we could replace our existing overloaded political labels with a clearer list of the political outcomes we care about, and the metrics we believe impact those outcomes.

Note: This is a simplified demo, so the views below are incomplete, and there are implementation details of the idea not explored.

Outcome Selection

There are significant complexities in choosing a metric for an outcome. A benefit of my happiness metric is that it provides a good sense of what is realistic - it may not be possible (or wise) to try to maximize the absolute happiness number the UN calculates, but keeping up with the international standard seems reasonable.

A downside with the metric is that a significant decrease in happiness experienced equally in all countries would not change the metric, but might still be relevant to me. To catch this, I could add a floor-based metric using the absolute happiness number.

Some outcomes that I care about will not have an appropriate metric. Even in this situation, the system is still useful because at least the lack of an appropriate metric is made clear.

Impacting Metrics

For each of the outcomes that I care about, the system can display other metrics that impact it. I believe that the economy's growth rate has an impact on unemployment - a higher growth rate leads to a lower unemployment rate. The growth rate metric I will use is the difference between the growth rate and the average growth rate for other advanced economies (similar to my happiness metric, this provides a sense of what is possible). I'll also go one level deeper and show three of the metrics that I believe impact the growth rate.

I only actually care about the outcomes in the top row, everything below that is a means to those ends. This structure makes my political view clearer.

I'll also add metric forecasts - try changing the year drop-down.

Note that this is an incomplete dashboard, a full political view would be larger and more complex.

A few notes:

  • This sample projection only shows first and second order effects.
  • The dashboard is incomplete.
  • Only one projection is displayed, but it's possible to show projections from multiple groups, along with information such as the accuracy of the groups over time and which projection the dashboard owner believes.
  • The projection only displays the direction of the change (i.e. if it will get better or worse), but it's possible to also show actual numerical projections.
  • A projection may include metrics that the dashboard being viewed does not include (e.g. maybe the projection above also states that trade will be impacted, but John Smith doesn't care about trade). The dashboard could display these in a separate section.

Consistency

In our current system of political discussion, the best case is a well-written article that outlines the author's political view. But even in this case, we're still dealing with flat text that makes it hard to quickly understand the view and compare it to others. And the vast majority of political discourse is far from this ideal case. For example, when debating policy people are often talking about different things, or at different levels. The consistency provided by having views presented in a dashboard would make political discussion much clearer and easier to understand.

Who Does What

The three types of content in a dashboard are:

  • The outcomes the person cares about
  • For each of those outcomes, the other policy metrics that impact it
  • The various projections on the impact of a proposed policy change, and which projections the view holder believes

Policy-minded people already know those things, so in v1 their existing views would be converted into a dashboard.

The system would automatically display the most recent data for the various metrics.

Less policy-minded people may know the outcomes they care about, but not necessarily all of the impacting metrics and projections. Over time the system itself could provide the impacting metrics and projections, to provide use cases to those people.
